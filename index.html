ABOUT JSS

JSS Institute of Speech and Hearing , Mysuru is a unit of JSS Mahavidyapeetha. The institute is affiliated to the University of Mysore and recognized by the Rehabilitation Council of India, Govt. of India. The Institute is also recognized as research center by University of Mysuru. The activities of the institute are spread over in four major domains like academic, research, clinical and public education. The Institute has been offering Bachelors, Maters and Doctoral Programs in Speech and Hearing. Seminars/ CRE / Workshops are regurarly conducted to upgrade the knowledge & skills of in-service, master trainers & practicing professionals in the field.

ABOUT CONFERENCE

The National Conference on Community-Based Rehabilitation (CBR) is a significant gathering that serves as a platform for stakeholders, experts, policymakers, and practitioners from across the country to converge and discuss critical issues related to disability inclusion, rehabilitation, and community development. The aim of the conference is to empower delegates on enhancing the knowledge and skills necessary to effectively address the unique needs of persons with disabilities (PWDs), thereby fostering greater inclusivity within our society. The following objectives were framed

  • a) Determine the readiness and capacity of the institute to establish a CBR unit.
  • b) Promote collaboration (professionals, government officials, NGOs, educators and engineers) to facilitate coordinated approach to CBR implementation.
  • c) To provide capacity building sessions among rehabilitative professionals to develop an advocacy strategy to garner support for the CBR unit at the institutional and community level and
  • d) To identify potential funding sources and sustainability strategies for the unit.

The outcome of national conference is to equip rehabilitative professional and stakeholders to improve knowledge and skill development necessary to effectively install and implement a community-based rehabilitation (CBR) program at institutional and community level.

Who all can participate?

Undergraduate students, postgraduate students, research scholars, and working professionals from diverse specialties, including speech-language pathology, audiology, special education, physiotherapy, occupational therapy, psychology, and social work, are actively engaged in this academic community
